📖 In-app guide — Stage 1 Reset
Breaking the banking catch-22
How to open a UK bank account with no credit history, no UK proof of address, and no UK references — and exactly which accounts accept which documents
What the catch-22 is
The classic banking catch-22 that almost every new UK immigrant hits: you cannot open a bank account without proof of address, and you cannot get proof of address without a bank account. You cannot rent without a bank account. You cannot get paid without a bank account. You cannot build credit without a bank account.

This guide breaks every part of that cycle — in the right order, with the right accounts for your specific document situation.
The sequence matters. Do not try to open a credit card before you have a current account. Do not try to open a high-street bank account before you have a proof of address. Follow this guide in order and the system unlocks.
Step 1 — Open a bank account that accepts your immigration documents
Not all UK banks accept immigration documents as the sole form of ID and address. The accounts below do — choose the one that matches what you currently have.
Monzo
Digital bank — personal account
Accepts passport or national ID + eVisa or BRP. No UK address required to apply — you can use a friend's address initially. Opens in under 10 minutes. Free card, instant notifications, no monthly fee.
Accepts eVisa / BRP
Starling Bank
Digital bank — personal account
Accepts passport + proof of right to live in UK (eVisa or BRP). Fully regulated UK bank. Free account, no fees for foreign currency spending. Strong app. Opens in 24–48 hours.
Accepts eVisa / BRP
Suits Me
e-money account — no credit check
Specifically designed for people with no UK credit history. Accepts ARC card (for asylum seekers), BRP, eVisa. No credit check required. Not a full bank account but functions as one for day-to-day use. Small monthly fee.
Accepts ARC card
Bloom Money
Diaspora savings and banking
FCA-authorised rotating savings (ajo, susu, chit fund) for diaspora communities. Accepts immigration documents. Builds community financial relationships alongside a basic account. Particularly relevant if you are not yet comfortable with mainstream banking.
Community-first
Wise (formerly TransferWise)
Multi-currency account
Accepts passport + immigration documents. Excellent for receiving money from overseas and converting currencies. Has a UK sort code and account number so can be used as a main account. Strong with migrant communities for international transfers.
Accepts eVisa / BRP
Halifax / Lloyds / Barclays
High-street banks
These require more documentation and typically a UK address. Come back to these once you have 3–6 months of banking history with a digital bank. They offer more products (overdrafts, mortgages) and having one matters for your long-term credit profile.
Step 2 — not yet
→

Action: open your first account today

Download Monzo or Starling if you have an eVisa or BRP. Download Suits Me if you have an ARC card. Do this before Step 2. You need a bank account in place before the rest of the sequence works.

Step 2 — Get proof of address
1

Register to vote (electoral roll) — if eligible

Go to gov.uk/register-to-vote. Eligibility: British citizens, EU citizens with settled/pre-settled status, and some Commonwealth citizens. Not available to most non-EU immigrants — but if you are eligible, do this immediately. Electoral roll registration is one of the fastest ways to build a credit profile and get proof of address.

→ Takes 5 minutes. Appears on your credit file within 4–6 weeks.
2

Use your bank statement as proof of address

Once you have had your Monzo or Starling account open for 1–3 months, you can download a bank statement showing your name and address. This becomes your proof of address for almost all other applications — landlords, phone contracts, other bank accounts.

→ Download after your first full month. Save as PDF. You will use this often.
3

HMRC letters and utility bills

Once you are registered for National Insurance (apply at gov.uk/apply-national-insurance-number) and paying council tax or utility bills, these letters count as proof of address. Keep every letter you receive from HMRC, your local council, and utility providers.

Step 3 — Get a National Insurance number
→

Apply as soon as you arrive

Apply online at gov.uk/apply-national-insurance-number. You need your passport and immigration document (eVisa or BRP). Your NI number is required by every employer before you can be paid through PAYE, and it is referenced in your tax records, benefits claims, and pension contributions. Without it you can still work — but you should apply immediately and chase up if you do not receive it within 8 weeks.

→ Keep your NI number memorised. Write it somewhere safe. You will need it constantly.
Accounts to avoid — and why
Payday lenders and high-interest credit: Wonga, QuickQuid, and similar payday lenders are closed, but successors exist. Any lender offering credit at APR above 30–40% should be avoided. The short-term relief is not worth the debt trap, and defaulting on high-interest credit damages your credit file for 6 years.

Unregulated "community banking" schemes: Some informal money-keeping arrangements exist in diaspora communities. These are not FCA-regulated. If something goes wrong, you have no legal protection. Use FCA-authorised institutions only. Check at register.fca.org.uk before trusting any financial provider.
The full sequence — summary
1

Open a digital bank account this week

Monzo, Starling, or Suits Me depending on your documents. This is the foundation everything else builds on.

2

Register to vote if eligible

5 minutes at gov.uk/register-to-vote. One of the highest-impact things you can do for your credit profile.

3

Apply for your National Insurance number

gov.uk/apply-national-insurance-number. Required for work, benefits, and everything official.

4

After 1–3 months: get a credit-builder card

See the UK Credit Building Guide for this step. Do not skip ahead — you need banking history first.

5

After 6 months: consider a high-street bank account

Halifax, Lloyds, or Barclays once you have a banking history to show them. Unlocks overdrafts, better products, and stronger credit profile.
