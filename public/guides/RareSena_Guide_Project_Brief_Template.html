The RareSena Project Brief
One page that turns an intention into a completed thing — fill it in, print it, pin it where you work
Why one page
Projects die from vagueness, not difficulty. "Work on my portfolio" survives forever as an intention because it can never be finished — nothing defines what finished means. This brief exists to kill vagueness in seven questions. If you cannot answer all seven, you do not yet have a project — you have a wish. Answering them takes fifteen minutes and doubles the probability the thing gets done.
1. Project name
Name the finished thing, not the activity.
✎ "UK-formatted CV, reviewed and in use" — not "CV work"
2. Done means…
The completion test. Someone else could verify it without asking you anything.
✎ "Done = CV reviewed by one professional in my sector, all Task 4.3 checklist items ticked, and 3 applications submitted using it."
3. Why this project — the direction link
One sentence connecting it to your 90-day direction from Task 4.1. If you cannot connect it, it is a distraction wearing a project costume.
4. Completion date
A date, in your calendar, told to one other person.
5. The first action (this week) and the weekly slot
The very next physical action, and the recurring time block where this project lives (tie it to your Task 3.1 routine).
✎ "First action: draft work-experience bullets Saturday 9am. Weekly slot: Tuesday + Thursday 6:30–7:15am."
6. What could stop me — and the counter-move
Name the most likely failure mode now, while you are strong, and decide the response in advance.
✎ "Obstacle: perfectionism loop on wording. Counter-move: 45-minute timer per section; done beats perfect; reviewer catches what matters."
7. Evidence and accountability
What proof will exist, and who sees it — post the commitment in Rare Circle Rise for the completion-rate boost of a public deadline.
The four rules of the brief
1.
Shrink until frightening becomes doable. If the completion date is more than 6 weeks out, the project is too big for Stage 4 — cut scope until it fits. You can always run a second project.
2.
One project at a time. Two half-finished projects are worth less than one finished one — completion is the asset, not activity.
3.
The brief is allowed to change; the date is not. Scope can shrink mid-project. Moving the date needs a reason you would say out loud in Rare Circle.
4.
Review it in your weekly review. Task 3.3, dimension five — "one thing forward" — is where this brief gets its weekly heartbeat.
