Is my qualification recognised in the UK? The complete sector guide
Every major profession: the regulator, the process, the realistic cost, and the realistic timeline — so you plan from facts, not forum rumours
Start here — three different questions people confuse
Which of these is actually your question?
1. "Is my degree equivalent to a UK degree?" → UK ENIC Statement of Comparability. ~£49.50 + VAT, 15 working days. Needed for employers, universities, and most professional registrations. Apply at enic.org.uk.
2. "Can I legally practise my profession here?" → Only relevant for regulated professions (below). If your profession is regulated, ENIC alone is NOT enough — you need registration with the UK regulator.
3. "Will employers value my experience?" → Not a legal question at all. This is translation — UK CV, professional summary, networking (Tasks 2.2 and 4.3 cover it).
Check whether your profession is regulated at all: gov.uk/guidance/regulated-professions-in-the-uk keeps the official list. Most jobs in the UK — including most business, tech, creative, and administrative roles — are NOT regulated. If yours is not on the list, you can work immediately; your task is translation, not registration.
Regulated professions — sector by sector
🩺
Medicine (doctors)
Regulator: General Medical Council (GMC) — gmc-uk.org. International medical graduates typically qualify via the PLAB route (or an accepted postgraduate qualification).
  • English evidence (IELTS 7.5 overall or OET grade B typical requirement)
  • PLAB 1 (written, sittable overseas) → PLAB 2 (clinical OSCE, Manchester)
  • GMC registration with licence to practise → most IMGs start in FY2-equivalent or trust-grade posts
PLAB 1+2 ≈ £1,300 + GMC feesTypically 12–24 months end to end
💉
Nursing and midwifery
Regulator: Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) — nmc.org.uk. Overseas-trained nurses complete a two-part Test of Competence.
  • English evidence (IELTS 7.0 / OET B — combined sittings allowed within rules)
  • CBT (computer-based theory test — sittable in your home country)
  • OSCE (practical exam at an approved UK test centre) — many employers sponsor and fund this after hiring you on a pre-registration basis
CBT ~£83 · OSCE ~£794 · NMC registration £153/yr6–18 months; faster with an NHS employer sponsor
🦷
Dentistry
Regulator: General Dental Council (GDC) — gdc-uk.org. Non-EEA qualified dentists sit the Overseas Registration Exam (ORE), Parts 1 and 2. Demand for exam places significantly exceeds supply — book the moment windows open.
ORE Part 1 ~£806 · Part 2 ~£2,929Commonly 2–4 years including waits
💊
Pharmacy
Regulator: General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC) — pharmacyregulation.org. Non-EEA pharmacists complete the OSPAP (one-year university diploma), then foundation training year, then the registration assessment.
OSPAP tuition ~£10,000–£15,000~2.5–3 years total
⚖️
Law (solicitors)
Regulator: Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) — sra.org.uk. Overseas lawyers qualify through the SQE (SQE1 written + SQE2 practical). Qualified lawyers from some jurisdictions may get SQE2 exemptions — check the SRA's agreed exemptions list. Two years' qualifying work experience required (overseas legal experience can count).
SQE1 £1,934 + SQE2 £2,974 (plus prep courses)12–30 months depending on exemptions
🏫
Teaching
Regulator: Teaching Regulation Agency (England). Overseas-trained teachers from eligible countries can apply for QTS directly and free via apply-for-qts-in-england.education.gov.uk. Others route through assessment-only QTS or ITT. Scotland (GTCS), Wales (EWC), and NI have separate systems.
Direct QTS application: freeWeeks (direct route) to 12 months (assessment routes)
🏗️
Engineering
Not licence-regulated for most work — you can be employed as an engineer without registration. But CEng/IEng chartered status (via a licensed institution — ICE, IMechE, IET etc., under the Engineering Council) is the credibility currency for senior roles. Overseas degrees assessed individually; ENIC statement supports the application.
Work first, charter alongsideCEng review: 6–12 months once evidence ready
🤝
Social work
Regulator: Social Work England — socialworkengland.org.uk. International registration route assesses your qualification and experience against UK standards; English evidence required.
International application £675Up to 6 months assessment
📐
Architecture
Regulator: Architects Registration Board (ARB) — the title "architect" is legally protected. Overseas qualifications assessed for Part 1/2 equivalence; Part 3 (professional practice) usually completed in the UK. You can work in architectural roles (e.g. "architectural designer") while completing registration.
12–24+ months to full registration
📊
Accountancy
Not legally regulated for most roles (audit is the exception). ACCA, ICAEW, and CIMA all grant exemptions for overseas qualifications — check each body's exemption calculator with your exact credential. Many overseas-qualified accountants work immediately while converting.
Exemption calculators online — check before paying anything
The universal sequence — whatever your sector
1) Confirm whether your profession is regulated (gov.uk list). 2) If yes: identify the regulator and read their international route page directly — never rely on agents or forums for requirements. 3) Apply for your ENIC statement in parallel, not after. 4) Sit English tests early — they expire (usually 2 years) but they gate everything. 5) Budget realistically using the figures above and add them to your Task 3.5 financial goals. 6) Work adjacent while you convert — healthcare assistants, paralegals, architectural designers, and unqualified-teacher roles all keep you in your field, earning, and building UK references.
Scam warning: no legitimate regulator uses third-party "fast-track registration agents." Anyone offering guaranteed registration, exam leaks, or paid shortcuts is defrauding you. Every process above can be done directly with the regulator.
