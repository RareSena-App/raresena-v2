Income options by visa type — the complete 8-track reference
What is permitted, what is conditional, what is prohibited — and the specific actions that trigger a compliance breach on each route
Read this first: this guide states the general rules of each route. Your individual visa conditions are what legally bind you — check your eVisa/grant letter, because individual conditions can vary. For any grey area (especially anything resembling self-employment on a restricted route), get advice from an OISC-regulated adviser BEFORE acting, not after. A visa breach can cost you everything the rebuild has built.
A
Skilled Worker
✓ Permitted
  • Full-time work for your sponsor in your CoS role
  • Supplementary employment up to 20 hrs/week (eligible occupation, outside sponsor hours)
  • Personal savings interest, ISAs, investment income
  • Voluntary (genuinely unpaid) work
⚠ Conditional
  • Second job beyond supplementary rules → needs a second sponsorship/CoS update
  • Occasional one-off income (e.g. selling personal items) — fine; a repeated trade is not
✗ Prohibited
  • Self-employment / freelancing
  • Running your own business as an activity
  • Contracting through your own limited company
  • Regular trading income (Etsy shops, paid UGC, client services)
Breach triggers: invoicing anyone as yourself; a repeated pattern of trading income; supplementary work exceeding 20 hrs or in an ineligible occupation. Route out: after ILR, all restrictions end — this is a core reason the ILR date anchors your sovereignty plan.
B
Student
✓ Permitted
  • Employment up to 20 hrs/week in term time (degree level; 10 hrs below)
  • Full-time work in official vacations
  • Work placements that are part of the course
  • Savings interest and ISAs
⚠ Conditional
  • Post-study: Graduate Route visa opens near-unrestricted work incl. self-employment — plan for it now
✗ Prohibited
  • Self-employment of any kind
  • Business activity
  • Professional sportsperson roles
  • Exceeding weekly hours — even once
Breach triggers: hour breaches are the classic one — universities and the Home Office both check. Two part-time jobs that TOTAL over 20 hrs in term time is a breach. Know your official term dates in writing.
C
Family / Spouse / Partner
✓ Permitted
  • Any employment, any hours, any sector
  • Self-employment and freelancing
  • Running your own limited company
  • Digital income — UGC, products, courses
  • All investment income
⚠ Conditional
  • Public funds — most family routes carry NRPF; claiming prohibited benefits is a serious breach (working and earning is fine)
✗ Prohibited
  • Effectively nothing on the work side
Admin duties, not restrictions: register with HMRC for self-assessment if self-employed (before earning over the £1,000 trading allowance); keep clean records — your income also supports the financial requirement at your next extension.
D
BN(O) Hong Konger
✓ Permitted
  • Any employment, any sector
  • Self-employment and business ownership
  • Creator and digital income
  • Investment income; access to public funds (route allows recourse)
⚠ Conditional
  • Long absences affect ILR continuity — plan Hong Kong visits within the 180-day rolling rule
✗ Prohibited
  • Nothing significant on the income side
Focus: with no work restrictions, your constraint is purely strategic — build income that also builds the 5-year ILR record (steady employment history, clean tax record).
E
Refugee / Humanitarian Protection / Asylum
✓ Permitted (after status granted)
  • Any employment — same rights as a citizen
  • Self-employment and business
  • Universal Credit and in-work benefits
  • All digital and investment income
⚠ Conditional (while awaiting decision)
  • Generally no work until 12 months waiting, then only shortage-list roles on permission
  • Asylum support (~£49/week) is the lawful income meanwhile
  • Volunteering IS permitted while waiting — and builds UK references
✗ Prohibited
  • Working before permission while an asylum seeker — this can affect your case; do not risk it
The 28-day pivot: when status is granted, asylum support ends after the move-on period — apply for Universal Credit on day one, get the eVisa/BRP sorted, open full banking, and use Breaking Barriers (breaking-barriers.co.uk) for skilled employment support. Migrant Help: 0808 8010 503.
F
Global Talent
✓ Permitted
  • Employment for any employer — no sponsorship needed
  • Multiple simultaneous jobs
  • Self-employment, consultancy, freelancing
  • Directing your own companies
  • All digital, IP, speaking, and investment income
⚠ Conditional
  • ILR at 3 yrs (Exceptional Talent) or 5 yrs (Promise) requires earning in your endorsed field — keep evidence of continued activity in your field
✗ Prohibited
  • Professional sportsperson/coach roles only
Strategy: this is the most flexible work visa in the UK system. The optimal Stage 4/5 architecture is employment or consultancy anchor + portfolio income. Your only real constraint is keeping your field-activity evidence for settlement.
G
Innovator Founder
✓ Permitted
  • Running and taking income from your endorsed business (salary + dividends)
  • Supplementary employment up to 20 hrs/week (skilled-level role)
  • Investment income
⚠ Conditional
  • Everything is conditional on the endorsed business remaining your PRIMARY activity
  • Pivoting the business → discuss with your endorsing body first
✗ Prohibited
  • Employment becoming your main activity
  • Abandoning the endorsed venture while on the route
Compliance calendar: your contact-point meetings with the endorsing body are mandatory events — diarise them like visa expiry dates. ILR reminder: 3-year route; the £50,000 investment figure is one of the ILR milestone options (any two of four required) — it is NOT an entry requirement and NOT the only path to settlement on this route.
H
Self-Sponsored Skilled Worker (own company sponsors you)
✓ Permitted
  • Salary from your company in your CoS role (must meet threshold — £41,700 general rate as of July 2025, or the going rate for your SOC code)
  • Dividends as a shareholder in addition to salary
  • Investment income
⚠ Conditional
  • Supplementary employment: 20 hrs/week rules as Track A
  • Company pivots that change your role → CoS/licence implications; take advice first
✗ Prohibited
  • Salary falling below threshold (this is a visa breach, not just a business problem)
  • Your actual role materially differing from your CoS
  • Freelancing outside the company
The double book-keeping burden: you carry company compliance (sponsor licence duties, right-to-work records, reporting duties on the SMS system) AND personal compliance (salary, role). An accountant experienced with sponsor-licence companies is not optional on this route — budget for one.
Universal rules — every track

Four rules that apply to everyone

1. Personal investment income is broadly safe. Savings interest, ISA growth, and personal investment returns are not "work" — they are permitted on every route (you must be UK tax-resident for ISAs; capital at risk is your risk).

2. Tax compliance is visa compliance. HMRC records are checked at settlement. Register for self-assessment where required, declare trading income over £1,000/year, keep the record clean — a tax discrepancy at ILR is a self-inflicted wound.

3. Volunteering (genuinely unpaid) is permitted on every route — and it is the underrated income strategy: UK references, UK experience, and networks that convert into paid roles.

4. When a "side hustle" idea appears, run the test: is it repeated, organised activity for payment? Then it is probably self-employment — and on Tracks A and B that is a breach regardless of how small the money is. £30 of prohibited income can cost a visa worth everything. Check first, always.
