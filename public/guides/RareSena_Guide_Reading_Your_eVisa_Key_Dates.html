📖 In-app guide — Stage 1 Reset · Task 1.1
How to read your eVisa and find every key date
Your immigration status is now a screen, not a card. This guide walks every field, decodes what it means, and shows you where each date that governs your life actually lives
What an eVisa is — and why paper is gone
The UK has moved to a digital immigration system. Biometric Residence Permits (BRPs) stopped being issued and expired as physical proof at the end of 2024 — your status now lives online as an eVisa: a digital record attached to your UKVI account, viewed at gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status. There is no card to carry. When an employer, landlord, or bank needs proof, you generate a time-limited share code and they check it against the Home Office system directly. If you do not yet have a UKVI account, the companion guide — How to create your UKVI account step-by-step — comes first; this guide assumes you can log in.
Reading the screen — every field decoded
Field on your eVisaWhat it actually means for you
Name, date of birth, nationality, photoMust match your current passport exactly. A mismatch (old passport, transliteration difference, missing middle name) causes failed right-to-work and right-to-rent checks — fix via "Update your details" the day you spot it.
Status / Leave typeYour route — e.g. Skilled Worker, Student, Partner, Refugee, Global Talent, Innovator Founder. This determines which RareSena track (A–H) you follow and which income rules apply (see the Income Options by Visa Type guide).
Valid from / Valid until"Valid until" is THE date — your visa expiry. Everything in Task 1.1 keys off it. "Valid from" matters too: it usually starts your qualifying-period clock (see key dates below).
ConditionsThe legally binding lines: work permissions or restrictions ("Work permitted", "No public funds", study restrictions, police registration historically). "No public funds" (NRPF) means claiming most benefits is a breach — read your conditions word by word once, so nothing surprises you.
Share code generatorCreates codes for employers (right to work), landlords (right to rent), and general status proof. Codes last 90 days and show only what the checker is entitled to see.
Update your detailsWhere you change passport, email, phone, and address. Keeping these current is a legal requirement of holding an eVisa — and the practical requirement for never missing a Home Office letter.
Do this today (5 minutes): log in, screenshot every screen of your eVisa — profile, status, conditions — and save them dated in your ILR Evidence folder (folder 01, Identity & Immigration). The system occasionally has errors and outages; your dated screenshots are your record of what your status said and when. Repeat after every visa change.
The key dates — where each one lives and what it triggers
1. Visa expiry date ("Valid until")
On the main status screen. Enter it into the app's Visa & ILR Countdown Tracker now — the tracker generates your renewal milestones (start preparing ~6 months out; most renewals can be submitted up to 28 days before expiry without breaking continuity). Applying before expiry protects your rights while you wait (Section 3C leave); missing it by a day makes you an overstayer.
2. Your qualifying-period start date
Not shown as a labelled field — you derive it. It is normally the "Valid from" of the first visa in your current route (or your arrival date if you entered on a vignette). This starts the 5-year clock (3 years on some Global Talent and the Innovator Founder route) towards settlement. If you have switched routes, the counting rules change — this is a check-with-an-OISC-adviser question, answered once, definitively.
3. ILR eligibility date
Qualifying-period start + 5 years (or 3). The app's Visa & ILR Countdown Tracker calculates and displays it permanently. You can apply up to 28 days before this date. This is the date your Sovereignty Plan (Task 5.4) is built around.
4. Passport expiry — yours, not the visa's
Not on the eVisa at all, which is exactly why people miss it. Your eVisa is linked to your passport number; when you renew your passport you MUST update the eVisa via "Update your details", or border e-gates and share-code checks fail against the old number. Diarise your passport expiry alongside your visa dates.
5. Route-specific dates (Tracks G and H)
Innovator Founder: your endorsing-body contact-point meetings (roughly 12 and 24 months in) are mandatory — they live in your endorsement paperwork, not your eVisa. Self-Sponsored: your sponsor-licence reporting duties run on their own clock via the SMS system. Both belong in the Visa & ILR Countdown Tracker as custom dates.
Troubleshooting — the three common problems
1

The eVisa shows wrong information

Use "Report an error with your eVisa" (linked from the view-and-prove service) for status errors, or "Update your details" for personal details. Screenshot the error first — dated evidence, always. Errors do happen and are fixable; they do not change your underlying legal status.

2

You cannot log in

Recovery runs through the email/phone on the account — which is why keeping them current is non-negotiable. If recovery fails, the UKVI resolution centre (0300 790 6268, option 3 for eVisa problems) can restore access — have your reference numbers ready.

3

A share code check fails

Usually: expired code (regenerate — they last 90 days), passport mismatch (update details), or the checker using the wrong service. Generate the code fresh in front of the employer or agent if needed — it takes one minute.

→ Never send anyone your UKVI login. The share code IS the sharing mechanism — anyone asking for your login is either confused or dangerous.
Track E note: if you have just been granted status, your eVisa is your proof of refugee status and right to work — screenshot it immediately and generate a share code for Universal Credit and employers within the 28-day move-on window. If anything on it is wrong, call Migrant Help (0808 8010 503) the same day — errors during move-on cost time you do not have.
