Task 3.4 — Complete 30 consecutive days without breaking your streak
⏱ Daily log · Auto-completes at 30 days · All tracks
Why this matters
This task does not require you to do anything new. It requires you to keep doing what you have already committed to — every single day, for thirty consecutive days.

That sounds simple. It is not. Thirty consecutive days is long enough to encounter at least one bad week, at least one crisis, at least one day when everything feels too heavy for a morning routine and five habits. The value of this task is not the streak number. It is what you discover about yourself when the streak is threatened and you choose to protect it anyway.

This task auto-completes. There is no prompt, no submission, no button to press. When the streak tracker records 30 consecutive logged days, the task marks itself done and Stage 3 moves one step closer to complete. All you have to do is show up.
How auto-completion works: This task is connected to your in-app streak tracker from Tasks 1.4 and 3.2. Every day you log your habits, the streak counter increments. When it reaches 30 consecutive days, this task automatically marks as complete in your Supabase profile — no action required from you. Miss a day and the counter resets to zero. What to expect to feel
Around day 10 to 14 the novelty is gone and something harder sets in — the question of whether this is worth it when nothing has dramatically changed yet. Keep going. The change that thirty days of consistent habits creates is not dramatic. It is structural. You will not wake up on day 31 transformed. You will wake up as someone who has proved to themselves that they keep their commitments for thirty days. That proof is more valuable than almost anything else you could have done with those thirty days.
