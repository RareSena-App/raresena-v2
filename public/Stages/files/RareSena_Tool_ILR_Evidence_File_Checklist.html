ILR Evidence File Checklist
The file that builds itself over years — instead of being panic-assembled at month 58
Why this exists: ILR applications fail on evidence gaps more often than on eligibility. The applicants who struggle are not the ones who broke rules — they are the ones who cannot prove five years of compliant residence because nobody told them to keep the documents. Start this file today, whatever stage you are in. Tick items as you secure them. Your future self, sitting at the ILR application, will be working from a complete folder.

Golden rule: keep everything in at least two places — one physical folder and one cloud folder (clearly organised by year). A document you cannot find is a document you do not have.
🛂
Identity and immigration history
0/5
Current passport (and all previous passports covering your UK residence)
Old passports show your entry stamps and travel history — never discard them.
eVisa access confirmed / BRP records saved
Screenshot your eVisa status page periodically. Keep expired BRP card images.
All previous visa grant letters and decision letters
Every Home Office letter you have ever received — approvals, extensions, corrections.
Complete travel/absence log with dates
Use the Visa & ILR Countdown Tracker — export the absence log annually.
Police registration certificate (if it applied to your nationality historically)
The scheme ended in 2022 — but if you registered, keep the certificate.
💼
Employment and income
0/5
All payslips (monthly, every employer, every year)
Download PDFs monthly — payroll portals close when you leave an employer.
P60s for every tax year
Issued each April by your employer. One page per year proves annual income.
Employment contracts and job change letters
Especially important for Tracks A and H where role and salary are visa conditions.
Self-assessment returns and SA302s (if self-employed)
Download from your HMRC account after each filing.
Sponsor correspondence (Tracks A, H) or endorsing body records (Tracks F, G)
CoS references, sponsor letters, endorsement letters, checkpoint meeting records.
🏠
Residence and address
0/5
All tenancy agreements (every property, every renewal)
Continuous address history is core residence evidence.
Council tax bills or exemption letters (annual)
One per year per address. Councils can reissue but it takes weeks.
Utility bills — a sample from each year
Two or three per year is enough. Download PDFs; suppliers purge old accounts.
Bank statements — a sample from each year showing UK address and activity
Quarterly statements per year showing regular UK transactions.
GP registration and NHS records confirmation
Registration letters and appointment history support continuous residence.
📚
Application requirements
0/5
Life in the UK test — pass certificate
Book at gov.uk/life-in-the-uk-test (£50). Valid indefinitely once passed — take it early.
English language evidence (B1 minimum — degree taught in English or approved test)
Degree-holders: get your ENIC English proficiency confirmation alongside comparability.
ILR application fee saved (£3,029 per person — check current rate)
Fees change; verify at gov.uk before budgeting. Add this to your Task 3.5 financial goals.
Marriage/civil partnership certificate and relationship evidence (Track C and dependants)
Cohabitation evidence across the qualifying period: joint bills, joint tenancy, correspondence to the same address.
Children's documents (birth certificates, school letters) if applying with dependants
School attendance letters double as residence evidence for the whole family.
⚖️
Good character and compliance
0/4
Clean driving and court record — or full disclosure documents if not
Even minor issues must be declared. Non-disclosure is treated more seriously than the issue itself.
HMRC record clean — no outstanding tax issues
Check your personal tax account annually. Resolve discrepancies immediately.
No public funds claimed (where visa conditions prohibit)
If NRPF applies to your route, keep evidence you have not claimed — benefit records can be checked.
Regulated adviser review booked (recommended, 2–3 months before application)
A single review consultation is a fraction of the cost of a refused application. The Sovereignty Programme includes this.
