📈 Stage 4 — Rise
Task 4.2 — Identify and start your primary income stream
⏱ 45–60 min · Visa-compliant · All tracks
Why this matters
By Stage 4 you have a routine, a direction, and a financial tracking system. Now comes the question that determines whether the rebuild becomes sovereign or stays precarious: where is your income coming from, and is it building toward independence or deepening dependence?

Most immigrants in the UK reach Stage 4 with one income source — usually employment or a single employer — and no plan for what happens if that source disappears. A Skilled Worker who loses their sponsor. A student who graduates and enters the 60-day clock. A spouse whose relationship changes. A refugee whose Universal Credit is reviewed.

This task is about identifying the primary income stream that is right for your visa track and your stage — and taking the first concrete action to activate or grow it. Not a plan. An action. This week.

Track A — Skilled Worker: Your right to work is tied to your sponsor. You can work full-time for your sponsor and, in most cases, do supplementary work up to 20 hours/week in a second job — but that second job must not be your primary activity and must be in an eligible occupation. Self-employment is not permitted unless you switch visa routes.
💼
Primary sponsored employment
Your main role. Ensure salary meets threshold (£41,700 or going rate). This is your anchor income.
✓ Fully permitted
⏰
Supplementary employment
Up to 20 hours/week in a second job in an eligible occupation. Must not be primary activity.
⚠ Permitted with conditions
📦
Digital products
Selling digital products (templates, courses, guides) may be considered self-employment. Get regulated immigration advice before activating.
⚠ Seek advice first
📈
Career advancement
Promotion, salary increase, or lateral move to a stronger sponsor. Often the safest and fastest income growth path on this route.
✓ Fully permitted
Critical — Track A: Self-employment and running a business as your primary activity is NOT permitted on a Skilled Worker visa. If your income strategy involves self-employment, you need regulated immigration advice before proceeding. The consequences of a breach include visa curtailment.
Track B — Student: You can work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during official vacation periods. You cannot be self-employed or run your own business. Your primary income must come from permitted employment within these hours.
⏰
Part-time employment
Up to 20 hours/week during term. Track your hours meticulously — a breach risks your visa immediately.
✓ Permitted within limits
☀️
Vacation-period employment
Full-time work during official university vacation periods. Know your exact term dates and plan income accordingly.
✓ Permitted in vacations
🎓
Graduate Route planning
Start planning now for the Graduate visa (2-year open work permit after graduation). Your post-study income strategy starts here.
✓ Plan now, activate later
🏫
University hardship and bursaries
Most UK universities have hardship funds, bursaries, and scholarship opportunities for international students. Most go unclaimed. Apply.
✓ Not employment — apply
Track C — Family/Spouse: You typically have full right to work in the UK — employment, self-employment, and running a business are all permitted unless NRPF applies to your visa. Check your eVisa conditions carefully. If NRPF applies, this affects benefit eligibility but not your right to work.
💼
Full-time employment
No restrictions on employment type or hours. Focus on roles that use your skills at the appropriate level.
✓ Fully permitted
🛠️
Self-employment
Permitted. Register as self-employed with HMRC, get a UTR number, and keep clean financial records from day one.
✓ Fully permitted
🏢
Own business
You can incorporate and run your own UK limited company. No immigration restrictions on this route — just standard company law obligations.
✓ Fully permitted
📱
Digital income
UGC work, digital products, online courses, brand partnerships. All permitted. Declare income through self-assessment.
✓ Fully permitted
Track D — BN(O) Hong Konger: You have full right to work in the UK — employment, self-employment, business ownership. No restrictions. You also have access to public funds once you have leave to remain. Focus on income that builds toward ILR and the 5-year settlement milestone.
💼
Employment — unrestricted
Full right to work in any sector. Prioritise roles that contribute to your ILR qualifying period and build a strong employment record.
✓ Fully permitted
🏢
Self-employment or business
Permitted. Register with HMRC for self-assessment if self-employed. Incorporated companies welcome.
✓ Fully permitted
📱
Digital and creator income
UGC, brand deals, digital products, online courses. All permitted. Your bilingual background and cultural perspective is a creator asset.
✓ Fully permitted
Track E — Refugee/Asylum: If you have been granted refugee status or humanitarian protection, you have full right to work — the same as a UK citizen. If you are still awaiting a decision, you may be eligible for permission to work after 12 months. Your income options expand significantly once status is granted.
💼
Employment — post-grant
Full right to work in any sector once status is granted. No restrictions. Priority: employment that uses your skills and builds your UK work record.
✓ Fully permitted (post-grant)
🛠️
Self-employment
Permitted after grant of status. Register with HMRC as self-employed. Many refugees with professional skills start freelancing in their field during the registration period for their regulated profession.
✓ Permitted post-grant
🤝
Breaking Barriers programme
Specialist employment support for refugees. 71% of clients hold at least a degree. Employer partnerships, CV support, and interview coaching.
✓ Apply — breaking-barriers.co.uk
🏛️
Universal Credit in-work
Once working, you may be eligible for Universal Credit top-up depending on income. Use the Turn2us calculator to check eligibility.
✓ Permitted post-grant
Track F — Global Talent: You have maximum income flexibility. Employment, self-employment, business ownership, freelancing, consultancy, digital income — all permitted simultaneously, with no restrictions on sector or hours. Your visa was designed for this.
🎯
Consultancy in endorsed field
The highest-value use of Global Talent flexibility. Charge professional rates. Build a client roster. No employer tie.
✓ Fully permitted
💼
Employment plus portfolio income
Employment as anchor income, with consultancy, digital products, or speaking fees alongside. Maximum income architecture.
✓ Fully permitted
📦
Digital and IP income
Courses, books, tools, templates, speaking engagements. Income from your expertise that does not depend on presence. Global Talent visa is built for this.
✓ Fully permitted
🏢
Own company
You can incorporate, direct, and draw salary from your own company with no immigration restrictions.
✓ Fully permitted
Track G — Innovator Founder: Your visa requires your endorsed business to be your primary professional activity at all times. You can do supplementary employment up to 20 hours/week — but if your employed work becomes more prominent than your business, your endorsement is at risk.
🚀
Endorsed business — primary
This must be your primary professional activity. Focus on generating your first revenue. Your ILR milestones depend on business progress, not employment.
✓ Required — primary focus
⏰
Supplementary employment
Up to 20 hours/week maximum. Cannot become your primary activity. Use as a bridge while the business grows — not as a permanent income substitute.
⚠ Permitted — 20hr max only
💰
Business revenue streams
Products, services, B2B contracts, partnerships — all within your endorsed business. Your £1m revenue ILR milestone starts with your first invoice.
✓ Fully permitted — pursue actively
Track G critical reminder: Your endorsing body can withdraw your endorsement if they determine your business is not progressing. Your next mandatory contact point meeting is a compliance event — not optional. Know your date.
Track H — Self-Sponsored (own company): Your visa is tied to your own company's sponsor licence. Your salary from that company must meet the threshold (£41,700 as of July 2025) and your role must match your CoS. Income from the company is permitted — additional self-employment or work for other employers needs careful immigration advice.
🏢
Salary from own company
Your primary income. Must meet the £41,700 threshold. Pay yourself through PAYE. Keep payslips, contracts, and board minutes — the Home Office may request these.
✓ Required — maintain threshold
💰
Company dividends
Directors can take dividends in addition to salary. This is standard UK company practice — ensure your accountant structures this correctly.
✓ Permitted — get accountant advice
📈
Company revenue growth
Win clients, grow the business, increase revenue. This increases what you can legitimately pay yourself and builds the company value that supports ILR.
✓ Pursue actively
Track H critical reminder: If your company cannot sustain the minimum salary threshold, your visa compliance is at risk — not just your income. This is a legal obligation, not a financial aspiration. Get an immigration-specialist accountant involved early.

What to expect to feel
Income anxiety in Stage 4 is different from income anxiety in Stage 1. In Stage 1 it was survival anxiety — will I have enough to eat and pay rent? In Stage 4 it is direction anxiety — am I building the right thing, am I moving fast enough, is this actually going anywhere? Both are real. Both require action rather than reassurance. The action this task asks for is small and specific — one thing, this week. That is enough for now.
