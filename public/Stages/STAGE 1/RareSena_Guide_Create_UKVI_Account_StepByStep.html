📖 In-app guide — Stage 1 Reset · Task 1.1
How to create your UKVI account step-by-step
The account that holds your immigration status — set up once, correctly, in about 20 minutes
Do you actually need to do this?
Most people who applied for a UK visa recently already have a UKVI account — it was created during the application (you verified your identity with the "UK Immigration: ID Check" app). If that is you, you do not create a new account; you simply log in at gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status with the email you applied with. Creating an account from scratch mainly applies if you: held a BRP and never set up eVisa access, entered on an older visa or vignette, were granted status through a process that did not create one (some Track E grants), or genuinely cannot recover the original. One person = one account, forever. Duplicate accounts cause status-matching problems that take months to untangle — always attempt recovery before creation.
Before you start — the 5-item checklist
1.
Your current passport (or travel document) — physically with you, not a photo of it
2.
Your BRP card if you have one (even expired — the number links your existing status), or your visa application reference (GWF/UAN number from application emails)
3.
An email address you will keep for years — not a work email, not a university email that dies when you graduate. This is the recovery anchor for your immigration status
4.
Your own mobile number (UK or international format both work)
5.
A smartphone able to install the free "UK Immigration: ID Check" app (App Store / Google Play) — it reads the chip in your passport/BRP via NFC
The steps
1

Go to the official start point — and only the official one

Search gov.uk for "get access to your eVisa" or go directly to gov.uk/get-access-evisa. Everything happens on gov.uk and in the official app. Any other website offering to "set up your eVisa" for a fee is a scam — this process is free, always.

2

Create the UKVI account

Enter your identity details exactly as they appear in your passport — every name, in order, spelled identically. Enter your email and phone, then confirm both with the verification codes sent to each. These become your login and your recovery route.

→ The spelling rule matters more than it seems: the system matches you to your immigration records by these details. Mismatches are the #1 cause of "we cannot find your status".
3

Verify your identity with the ID Check app

The website hands you to the "UK Immigration: ID Check" app. It will: scan the photo page of your passport (or your BRP), read the chip by holding the document flat against the back of your phone (remove thick cases; hold still — it takes a few seconds), scan your face, and have you record a short liveness video. Results usually confirm quickly.

→ NFC trouble? Move the document slowly around the back of the phone to find the antenna, or borrow a different phone — the account is yours, not the phone's.
4

Link your status

With identity confirmed, the system connects your account to your immigration record — via your BRP number or application reference. Once linked, your eVisa appears when you sign in at view-and-prove.

5

Prove it works, then file the evidence

Sign in at gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status, check every detail against your documents, generate one test share code, and screenshot everything into your ILR Evidence folder (01 — Identity & Immigration). Now enter your visa dates into the app's Visa & ILR Countdown Tracker — which is exactly where Task 1.1 continues.

Problems and fixes

"Your details do not match our records"

Almost always a transliteration or name-order difference between your passport and your visa records. Try the exact format on your BRP/grant letter. If it persists, contact the UKVI resolution centre (0300 790 6268) — do not create a second account to get around it.

The ID Check app cannot read the chip

Remove the phone case, place the document flat against the upper back of the phone, and hold completely still. Some older BRPs have weak chips — if it repeatedly fails, the website route offers alternatives, including verification without the app in some cases.

You no longer control the email on your old account

Recover, do not recreate: the account recovery process (linked from the sign-in page) lets you update the email after identity checks. The resolution centre handles stuck cases.

Setting up for children or family members

Every family member needs their own account — a parent manages accounts for under-18s as a "proxy". Use one master email pattern you control and record which email belongs to which person in your document folder.

The standing duty: once your account exists, you are required to keep it current — new passport, new phone number, new email, new home address all go in via "Update your details" promptly. Ten minutes of admin per life change, and it is the difference between the Home Office reaching you and a missed letter you never knew existed. Diarise a check twice a year alongside your Task 3.3 review.
