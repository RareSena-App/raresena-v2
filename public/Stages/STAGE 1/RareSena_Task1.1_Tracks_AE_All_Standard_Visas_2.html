Task 1.1 — Understand your visa options and deadlines
⏱ 20–30 min  ·  🛡 Non-negotiable  ·  Tracks A–E
Why this matters
Your visa expiry date is not a suggestion — it is the thing that can undo everything you are building here. The system does not send you reminders. It does not care if you were busy, overwhelmed, or simply unaware. Most immigrants who miss critical immigration deadlines do not miss them because they were careless. They miss them because nobody told them to look on day one.

This task changes that. You are going to find every date, every condition, and every next action that applies to your specific visa — right now, before anything else gets in the way.

  1. Go to gov.uk/view-prove-immigration-status and log into your UKVI online account. Screenshot your eVisa status page and save it.
  2. Note your visa expiry date. Calculate your 60-day window: if your employer's sponsor licence were revoked today, you would have 60 days to find new sponsorship or leave. Know this number.
  3. Check your visa conditions: right to work, restrictions on job type or employer, whether NRPF applies. Write these down.
  4. Set four calendar reminders: 6 months before expiry (start renewal research), 3 months before (submit application), 1 month before (urgent action), 2 weeks before (final check).
  5. Find your employer's HR contact name and save it. In a Skilled Worker emergency, they are your first call.

  2. Confirm your eVisa is linked to the correct passport. If you renew your passport, update the linkage in your UKVI account before any travel. Failure causes "no-board" errors at airports.
  3. Note your 5-year settlement date and the 180-day absence rule: you cannot spend more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month period during the 5-year qualifying period.

Track E — time-sensitive: If you have just received a positive asylum decision, your move-on clock starts now. You have approximately 28 days before asylum support ends. Contact Migrant Help (0808 8010 503) today.
  1. If awaiting a decision: Locate your ARC card. If lost, report to the Home Office via Migrant Help: 0808 8010 503 (free, 24/7).
  2. If just granted status: Contact Migrant Help on the day of your grant letter. Within 7 days: contact local council housing, apply for Universal Credit, open a bank account using your BRP or eVisa, register with a GP.
  3. Note your BRP expiry date or activate your eVisa. Your status is tied to this document — keep it safe.
  4. If you have an active appeal: contact a regulated immigration adviser. Free legal aid is available for some asylum appeals. The Refugee Council (refugeecouncil.org.uk) can help find accredited support at no cost.
What to expect to feel
Reading through visa conditions can trigger a specific anxiety — the feeling that your entire life here rests on a document. That feeling is real and reasonable. The system was designed to be managed, not to be kind. What this task does is move you from passive anxiety to active knowledge. Knowing your dates does not remove the pressure — but it removes the thing that makes pressure dangerous: not knowing.
